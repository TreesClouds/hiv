What is HIV

HIV is an acronym for Human Immunodeficiency Virus and AIDS is also an acronym for Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome. HIV and AIDS are connected in a way but they arenâ€™t the same thing, HIV is an infection that leads to the possibility of getting AIDS which is a condition.

How It Spreads

HIV is spread by certain body fluids, and when it is spread it is called HIV transmission and the body fluids it can be spread by is...

  1. Blood
  2. Semen
  3. Pre-Semeinal Fluids
  4. Vaginal Fluids
  5. Breast Milk

    6. and etc...

The main ways that HIV spreads through actions is because of...

  1. Engaging in anal or vaginal sex with another individual(s) who has HIV without using a condom or taking medicine to prevent or treat HIV
  2. Sharing a needle (most likley for drugs) with another individual that has HIV without it being 100% clean

Medication to Prevent & Treat HIV

PrEP

PrEP is medication that can prevent you from getting HIV and usually people who have a very high chance of getting it take it. It's a pill you take everyday, and the pills (brand) name is Truvada. It contains to medicine that treat HIV in that singular pill

Treatment Once You Have It

There isn't any cures at this time for HIV, but hopefully in the future there will be. But... what they do have at this time is treatment for it that helps you not spread it and that can dramatically slow the disease's progress, which prevents symptoms and other consequences that can occur later in your life. Even though you probably can't pronounce these names, examples of the more popular HIV medication are...

  1. abacavir (Ziagen)
  2. abacavir/lamivudine (Epzicom)
  3. abacavir/lamivudine/zidovudine (Trizivir)
  4. lamivudine/zidovudine (Combivir)
  5. lamivudine (Epivir)
  6. zidovudine (Retrovir)
  7. emtricitabine (Emtriva)
